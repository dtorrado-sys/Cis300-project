Hair Guide

Know your hair. Love your hair.

Four expert tips curated for every hair type. Whether your hair is dry, color-treated, heat-styled, or struggling at the scalp — we have something for you.

Tip 01

For dry hair

Dry hair is thirsty hair — and it needs more than just a quick rinse of conditioner. The key is to layer moisture and give it time to absorb before washing it away.

Apply the Laen Conditioner generously on damp hair from mid-length to ends. Let it sit for a full three minutes before rinsing with lukewarm water. The AHA complex in the formula works best when given time to penetrate the cuticle, so patience here pays off visibly.

For an intensive weekly treatment, swap the conditioner for the Laen Treatment and leave it on for five to ten minutes under a shower cap. You will feel the difference from the very first use.

Tip 02

Scalp care

A healthy scalp is the foundation of healthy hair. Most people focus entirely on the lengths and ends — but if the scalp is congested, irritated, or out of balance, no amount of conditioning will fix the result.

When applying the Laen Shampoo, use your fingertips — not your nails — and massage in slow, circular motions for at least sixty seconds before rinsing. This stimulates circulation, loosens buildup, and allows the AHA complex to do its work on the scalp surface.

Avoid washing with water that is too hot. Hot water strips the scalp of its natural oils and can lead to overproduction of sebum, which makes hair look greasy faster than it should.

Tip 03

Color-treated hair

Color-treated hair has an open cuticle that loses pigment faster when exposed to heat, friction, and harsh formulas. The goal is to seal the cuticle as quickly as possible after washing and keep it sealed between washes.

Always finish your rinse with cool or cold water. It sounds simple, but it is one of the most effective things you can do to extend the life of your color. Cool water closes the cuticle, locks in moisture, and adds a noticeable shine to the hair surface.

Avoid washing color-treated hair more than three times per week. Between washes, use a dry shampoo at the roots to absorb oil without disturbing the color at the lengths.

Tip 04

Heat protection

Heat styling — blow drying, flat ironing, curling — strips moisture from the hair shaft and can cause structural damage to the protein bonds inside each strand over time. The right pre-styling routine makes a measurable difference.

Apply the Laen Treatment to damp hair before reaching for any heat tool. The AHA complex forms a light protective layer over the hair shaft that helps buffer the strand against high temperatures and reduces the visible frizz and breakage that come with daily heat styling.

Keep your tools at the lowest effective temperature and always move them in a continuous motion — holding heat in one spot is where the most damage occurs.
